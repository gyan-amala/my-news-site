Global Trade Accord Signed by 15 Nations to Lower Supply Chain Barriers

By Sarah Jenkins • Economy Bureau • 2026

In a landmark development for the international markets, representatives from fifteen countries finalized the Global Trade Resiliency Accord early this morning.

The agreement outlines a aggressive schedule to reduce tariffs by up to 12% on electronic components, raw lithium, and processed agricultural goods over the next eighteen months. Economists predict this move could unlock billions in stagnant market liquidity.
