Supreme Court Establishes Strict Strictures on Consumer Data Retention

By Marcus Vance • Polity Correspondent • 2026

In a historic, unanimous 9-0 ruling, the Supreme Court has fundamentally redefined the digital boundaries of personal privacy in the modern era.

The court held that corporate entities can no longer indefinitely warehouse consumer geolocation history without explicit, opt-in consent updated on an annual basis. The decision represents the largest shift in privacy jurisprudence in two decades.
