In a landmark development for the international markets, representatives from fifteen countries finalized the Global Trade Resiliency Accord early this morning.

The agreement outlines an aggressive schedule to reduce tariffs by up to 12% on electronic components, raw lithium, and processed agricultural goods over the next eighteen months. Economists predict this move could unlock billions in stagnant market liquidity.
