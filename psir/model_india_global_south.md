## Question Focus
*Evaluate the structural transformations in India's foreign policy as it positions itself as the voice of the Global.*

---

## Model Answer Framework

### Introduction
In the contemporary multi-aligned world order, India has successfully revitalized its traditional identity from a passive member of the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) into an assertive, leading "Voice of the Global South." This shift reflects a move from defensive isolation toward constructive global agenda-setting.

### Key Pillars of India's Global South Leadership

1. **Global Agenda-Setting:** During its G20 Presidency, India effectively championed the permanent inclusion of the African Union, forcing Western powers to shift their focus toward development issues, debt vulnerabilities, and climate finance.
2. **Digital and Physical Public Goods:** By exporting open-source architectures like India Stack (UPI) and spearheading the International Solar Alliance (ISA), New Delhi offers affordable, sustainable alternatives to developing nations without trapping them in predatory debt cycles.
3. **Strategic Autonomy:** India maintains a balanced stance between Western blocs (e.g., QUAD) and Eurasian partnerships (e.g., BRICS), ensuring that the economic anxieties of developing countries are not sidelined by East-West geopolitical conflicts.

### Challenges to India's Leadership
* **The China Factor:** Beijing's deep financial reach via the Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) competes directly for influence across Asia and Africa.
* **Economic Constraints:** Unlike China, India has domestic resource limitations that sometimes cause delays in delivering international infrastructure projects.

### Conclusion
India's leadership of the Global South is built on functional, developmental partnerships rather than rigid ideological blocks. To sustain this momentum, India must continue delivering reliable projects on time and bridging the gap between major global powers and developing nations.
